    How to use the Public Register

    ‘This Register is the official public record of the FSRA that shows details of firms, individuals and other bodies that are, or have been, approved by the FSRA. It also contains details relating to securities, funds and certain other information. It is designed to help the public, investors, and market participants verify regulatory status and access basic information.

    Listed Entities must also disclose market information through the FSRA’s disclosure platform as required under Rule 2.8.3 of the Markets Rulebook’

    How to make a complaint

    A person wishing to lodge a complaint with the FSRA directly should complete and submit the FSRA Complaints Form. You can find out more about complaints received and assessed by the FSRA here

    The FSRA tries to ensure that the information on the Register is correct and up-to-date. However, we cannot guarantee its accuracy and do not accept liability for errors or missing information. Some material is sent to the FSRA by other parties who are responsible for supplying us with accurate information.
